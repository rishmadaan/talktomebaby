The Reader panel

The Reader panel is TalkToMeBaby's primary reading surface. It renders your document as a clean reading view where every word is individually clickable — separate from the source editor so you can listen and follow along without staring at code.

Dual-layer highlighting

Two highlight layers work together so you always know exactly where you are:

  • Sentence band — a background highlight spans the full sentence currently being spoken.
  • Word sweep — a moving highlight on top of the sentence band tracks the exact word being spoken as playback advances.

The colors for both layers can be customized from the in-reader settings panel (gear button). See In-reader settings panel below.

Click-to-jump

Click any word in the Reader panel to jump playback there and resume from that word immediately. No need to stop and restart — the jump is instant.

From the source editor: press alt+j while the source editor has focus to jump playback to the word at your cursor. This always starts playback even if the reader is currently paused.

Auto-scroll and Return to playback

Auto-scroll follows playback as long as you have not scrolled the Reader panel yourself. The moment you scroll away — to look something up, re-read a passage, or copy text — auto-scroll disengages.

A Return to playback pill appears in the corner of the Reader whenever you have scrolled away from the active position. Click it to snap back to the currently playing sentence and re-engage auto-scroll.

Player bar and speed

The player bar sits at the bottom of the Reader panel and contains:

  • Speed presets — 0.5, 0.75, 1.0, 1.25, 1.5, 1.75, and 2.0×.
  • Fine slider — covers the full 0.5–2.0 range for any value in between.
  • Pause/Resume — toggle playback without leaving the Reader.
  • Stop — stops playback and ends the session.
  • Gear (⚙) — opens the in-reader settings panel (see below).

Pitch is preserved at all speeds — no chipmunk effect at 2×, no unnatural deepening at 0.5×. Speed is applied locally (no re-synthesis), so changes are instant. The chosen speed is persisted globally and restored on the next read.

Speed is not inside the settings panel; it lives in the player bar.

In-reader settings panel

Click the gear (⚙) at the right end of the player bar to open the settings panel. Click the gear again, press Esc, or click the ✕ to close it. The gear shows a pressed state while the panel is open.

The panel slides in above the player bar with three sections:

Provider

One row per available provider, each with a short description, a "key required" badge where relevant, and a check mark (✓) on the active one. Click a row to switch.

  • macOS say only appears on macOS — it is hidden on Windows and Linux.
  • If a key-required provider has no stored key, switching to it prompts for the key first; cancel and the selection snaps back to the current provider.

Voice

A dropdown of the active provider's voices, with the current voice selected. Voices are cached per provider for the session and prefetched when a read starts, so the first open is usually instant. If a provider's voices are still loading you will briefly see a disabled "Loading voices…" option; the list fills in automatically.

Appearance

  • Font size — a +/− stepper (12–28 px) that applies instantly and persists across sessions.
  • Highlight colors — separate color pickers for the sentence band and the current word. Changes apply instantly. Reset to theme clears both back to the theme default.

Switching keeps your place and never auto-plays. Changing provider or voice reconfigures the reader in place — the Reader is not torn down and rebuilt. The session re-primes paused at your current sentence with the new voice. Press play when you are ready. No surprise audio. The settings panel stays open across the switch.

Command palette alternative

The settings panel is the primary way to change provider and voice, but the command palette mirrors it:

  • TalkToMeBaby: Select TTS Provider — marks the active provider with ✓ / "(current)"; reconfigures in place on change.
  • TalkToMeBaby: Select Voice — same active marker; reconfigures in place.
  • TalkToMeBaby: Set API Key — store an API key for ElevenLabs or Sarvam AI.

Editor sync

While a session is active, the source editor stays in sync with the Reader:

Feature Description
Sentence decoration The sentence currently being spoken is highlighted in the source editor, so you can glance at the raw file and know exactly where you are.
alt+j Press alt+j with the source editor focused to jump playback to the word at cursor. Always starts playback even if paused.
editorClickToJump setting Controls whether a plain click in the source editor triggers a jump. Values: off, alt-j (keyboard shortcut only, the default), or plain-click (any click during an active session).
Document edits mid-read If you edit the file while reading, playback pauses and a prompt appears offering to restart from the current position or stop. The session model becomes stale the moment the document changes.
